What Are Hallucinations?

Hallucinations occur when an AI generates false or misleading information as if it were true.

Why Do They Happen?

Training rewards confident answers over admitting uncertainty, leading models to "guess" instead of saying "I don't know."

Pattern Matching

Not true understanding or reasoning

No "I Don't Know"

Models prefer confident guesses

Always Verify

Cross-check critical information

What is Prompt Engineering?

Crafting Effective AI Instructions

Understanding Prompt Engineering

The process of carefully designing prompts to produce desired AI outputs

Definition

Prompt engineering is the process of carefully designing the prompts or instructions given to generative AI models to produce the desired outputs. Prompts act as guides that provide context and set expectations for the AI.

— Amazon AWS

Prevent Hallucinations

Avoid overly simple or complex outputs

Example with Instructions & Guardrails:

Create a single-page HTML website about raising a dog.

What:
- Single-page HTML website about raising a dog
- 5 main sections covering puppy basics
- Attached as an artifact is my outline for the content proper

Who:
- This is a gift for my brother and his wife (my sister-in-law)
- They're getting their first dog soon, a bichon frisé
- They have close to no prior pet experience

Where:
- Will be hosted on GitHub Pages
- Needs to be mobile-friendly

Why:
- Help them feel prepared without overwhelming them
- Give them practical, actionable advice
- Make it approachable and supportive

Instructions:
- Title: "How to Raise Train a Dog" with <del> tag
- 5 sections: puppy prep, bringing puppy home, feeding, health/vet, socialization
- Tone: warm and encouraging, like advice from a knowledgeable friend
- Length: 3-5 key points per section (no walls of text)
- Design: Modern, clean, warm earth tone color palette, mobile responsive

Guardrails:
- No formal "dog training manual" language
- No excessive detail or breed-specific advice
- No stock photos (use placeholders: Seb-1, Seb-2, etc.)
- No emojis

Level 5

Use XML Tags for Structure

Organize complex prompts with XML tags

Clear Structure

Prevents mixing instructions with examples

Readable

Easy for humans and AI to parse

Helps Claude

Specifically trained to recognize XML

Example XML Structure:

<task>

<task>
  <description>
    Create a single-page HTML website about raising a dog.
  </description>
  
  <context>
    <what>
      <format>Single-page HTML website</format>
      <topic>Raising a dog - puppy basics for first-time owners</topic>
      <sections>5 main sections</sections>
    </what>
    <who>
      <recipient>Gift for my brother and his wife (my sister-in-law)</recipient>
      <audience_context>Getting their first dog soon, a bichon frisé</audience_context>
      <experience_level>Close to no prior pet experience</experience_level>
    </who>
    <where>
      <deployment>GitHub Pages static hosting</deployment>
      <requirements>Mobile-friendly, responsive design</requirements>
    </where>
    <why>
      <purpose>Help them feel prepared without overwhelming them</purpose>
      <goals>
        <goal>Give them practical, actionable advice</goal>
        <goal>Make it approachable and supportive</goal>
      </goals>
    </why>
  </context>
  
  <instructions>
    <title>How to Raise <del>Train</del> a Dog (with "Train" crossed out using del tag)</title>
    <structure>
      <sections>
        <section>Puppy prep</section>
        <section>Bringing your puppy home</section>
        <section>Feeding</section>
        <section>Health/vet care</section>
        <section>Socialization</section>
        <section>Common mistakes</section>
      </sections>
      <section_length>3-5 key points maximum per section (no walls of text)</section_length>
    </structure>
    <tone>
      <voice>Warm and encouraging, like advice from a knowledgeable friend</voice>
      <avoid>Clinical or formal dog training manual language</avoid>
    </tone>
    <design>
      <style>Modern, clean aesthetic</style>
      <color_palette>Warm earth tones</color_palette>
      <responsive>Mobile responsive required</responsive>
      <navigation>Smooth scrolling between sections</navigation>
      <animations>Subtle scroll animations</animations>
    </design>
    <images>
      <placeholders>Use image placeholders named: Seb-1, Seb-2, etc.</placeholders>
      <constraint>Do not use stock photo suggestions</constraint>
    </images>
  </instructions>
  
  <guardrails>
    <exclude>
      <item>No formal "dog training manual" language</item>
      <item>No overwhelming excessive detail</item>
      <item>No breed-specific advice</item>
      <item>No stock photo suggestions</item>
      <item>No emojis</item>
    </exclude>
  </guardrails>
  
  <examples>
    <style_reference type="visual">
      <description>Tailwind demos for style reference</description>
      <url>https://catalyst-demo.tailwindui.com/</url>
      <url>https://catalyst-demo.tailwindui.com/events</url>
      <url>https://catalyst-demo.tailwindui.com/orders</url>
      <url>https://catalyst-demo.tailwindui.com/settings</url>
    </style_reference>
  </examples>
</task>

Important Guidelines

Use Claude Enterprise for company data/code. Customer data/code can not be fed. If unsure about approval, ask first.

You CAN use it to execute bespoke programming tasks for customer projects but you can not give it access to full customer code repos.
